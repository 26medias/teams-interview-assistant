# Senior AI Engineer — Agentic Systems

**Location:** Remote (North America / Europe)
**Type:** Full-time
**Level:** Senior (L5+)

---

## About the Role

We are looking for a Senior AI Engineer with deep, hands-on expertise in state-of-the-art agentic AI systems. You will be a core technical force behind the design, development, and productionization of multi-agent architectures that operate autonomously to solve complex, long-horizon tasks.

This is not a role for someone who has *used* LLM APIs. This is a role for someone who has *pushed them to their limits* — who has debugged agent loops at 2am, reasoned carefully about context window management, and built systems that remain reliable when models hallucinate, tools fail, or plans go sideways.

---

## What You'll Do

- **Architect and build** production-grade agentic pipelines: orchestrators, sub-agents, tool-use layers, memory systems, and planning modules
- **Own the full agentic loop** from task decomposition and prompt engineering through execution, error recovery, and output validation
- **Design multi-agent coordination systems** — agent meshes, hierarchical delegation, parallelism strategies, and inter-agent communication protocols
- **Evaluate and integrate** frontier models (GPT-4o, Claude 3.x, Gemini 1.5 Pro, Llama 3, Mistral, etc.) with rigorous benchmarking and cost/latency tradeoffs
- **Build and maintain RAG pipelines** including chunking strategies, embedding model selection, vector store tuning, and retrieval optimization
- **Instrument systems for observability** — traces, agent step logs, token accounting, failure rate dashboards
- **Contribute to agent evals** — build datasets, scoring rubrics, and automated regression suites for agentic behavior
- **Stay at the frontier** — track and rapidly prototype emerging research (CoT, ReAct, Reflexion, MCTS planning, mixture-of-agents, etc.)
- Collaborate with product and research to translate ambiguous requirements into robust AI-powered features

---

## What We're Looking For

### Required

- 4+ years of software engineering experience, with at least 2 years focused on LLM-based systems in production
- Deep expertise with **agentic frameworks**: LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen, custom orchestrators, or equivalent
- Proven experience building **tool-using agents** with reliable function calling, structured output parsing, and graceful error handling
- Strong understanding of **prompt engineering** at a systems level: prompt architecture, chain-of-thought elicitation, persona design, and context budgeting
- Hands-on experience with **RAG**: embedding pipelines, chunking strategies, hybrid search, re-ranking
- Working knowledge of **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** or comparable tool/resource abstraction layers
- Experience with **multi-step planning and task decomposition** in dynamic, unpredictable environments
- Proficiency in Python; comfort with async programming and concurrent agent execution
- Experience with at least one major vector store (Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant, pgvector)
- Familiarity with **agent evaluation methodologies** and building eval harnesses

### Strongly Preferred

- Experience with **Claude API** (tool use, prompt caching, extended context), OpenAI Assistants API, or Gemini function calling at scale
- Contributions to open-source agentic projects or published benchmarks
- Experience with **long-horizon task execution** (30+ step agent runs, multi-session memory, goal persistence)
- Knowledge of **fine-tuning and RLHF** workflows for domain-specific agent behavior
- Background in distributed systems or event-driven architectures applied to agent orchestration
- Experience integrating agents with real-world external APIs, databases, browsers, code execution sandboxes
